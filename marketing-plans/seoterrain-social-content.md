## TL;DR

- **One channel**: LinkedIn, founder personal profile.
- **One cadence**: 1–2 posts per week. No "post daily for the
  algorithm" hustle.
- **Four post archetypes**: teardown · pattern · owner-voice ·
  behind-the-scenes.
- **No carousels by default**; text-first. Carousel only when a
  before/after genuinely benefits.
- **Every post ends in one of two CTAs**: a public report URL, or the
  audit at `/dashboard/new`.
- **No** TikTok, Reels, Shorts, Twitter/X (until there's a shared
  report URL to anchor a thread), company page, engagement pods,
  follow-for-follow, AI-generated posts, dance-trend nonsense,
  build-in-public daily updates.

## 1 — Platform selection

### Why LinkedIn-only at launch

Mapping the skill's platform table against this audience:

| Platform | Skill default | Verdict for SEO Terrain |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | B2B thought leadership, 3–5x/week | ✓ Yes — but 1–2x/week, founder voice, no thought-leadership theatre |
| **Twitter / X** | Tech, real-time, community, 3–10x/day | ✗ No until there's a public report URL to anchor a thread; revisit Month 2 |
| **Instagram** | Visual brands, 1–2 posts + Stories daily | ✗ No — founder presence only, no business posting |
| **TikTok** | Brand awareness, younger audiences | ✗ No — wrong audience, wrong format for this JTBD |
| **Facebook** | Communities, local businesses | ⚠ Defer — possible Month 2 entry via NL category-specific groups, comment-only (not posting) |
| **YouTube** | Long-form education | ✗ No — wrong cadence for solo builder; revisit at 100+ audits when there's data to talk about |

**Single-channel discipline rule**: do not add a second platform
until LinkedIn produces at least 1 audit-attributed visit per post,
sustained for 4 weeks.

### Channel posture

- **LinkedIn = primary distribution** for trust-building, teardowns,
  and "Joost the indie freelancer" persona reach.
- **Email cold outreach = primary acquisition.** LinkedIn supports
  acquisition; it doesn't replace it.
- **Public report URLs = primary social proof.** Every LinkedIn post
  with real evidence embeds or references one.

## 2 — Posting cadence (realistic for a solo builder)

| Cadence axis | Setting | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Posts per week | 1–2 | Solo builder also running other businesses |
| Best days | Tue / Wed / Thu | Mon = inbox triage, Fri = low engagement |
| Best times (NL/EU) | 09:00–11:00 CET | When SMB owners scroll between morning ops |
| Posts per month, hard cap | 8 | Quality > frequency — never chase the algorithm |
| Real-time engagement window | < 4 hours from posting | Reply to every comment same-day |
| Daily engagement (outside post days) | 30 minutes max | 2 thoughtful comments + 0–2 DMs to new connections |

**Vacation rule**: skip a week rather than ship filler. Two posts of
mediocre filler do more damage to the brand than one extra week of
silence.

## 3 — Founder voice rules

The voice is **Mikey the operator who also runs Brickwise and
FactuurDirect** — not "SEO Terrain the brand". First-person "I", not
"we", almost everywhere except product copy.

### Five voice rules

1. **Observant, not promotional.** Every post should sound like
   something the founder noticed today, not something the marketing
   team wrote yesterday.
2. **Specific over expansive.** "Add the secondary GBP category 'Wine
   bar'" — not "leverage your business profile categorisation
   capabilities".
3. **Honest absence beats fake authority.** If we don't know
   something, say so. Don't pretend the audit reads PageSpeed when it
   doesn't.
4. **Plain Dutch / plain English.** No "synergy", no "10x", no
   "unlock", no "AI-powered".
5. **Earned numbers only.** Numbers cited must be from real audits we
   actually ran, or sources we cite. Never round up. Never invent
   testimonials.

### Banned words (auto-fail in post review)

`AI-powered` · `revolutionary` · `world-class` · `10x` · `unlock` ·
`disrupt` · `next-gen` · `cutting-edge` · `seamless` · `intuitive` ·
`platform` · `enterprise` · `synergy` · `growth-hack` ·
"guaranteed results" · "page-1 in 30 days" · "rank #1" · "skyrocket" ·
"powered by AI" · "smart insights" · 🚀 (the emoji)

### Sentence/paragraph rules

- Sentences short. Average 12–18 words.
- Paragraphs short. Maximum 3 lines on mobile LinkedIn.
- No em-dashes. Replace with a sentence break or a comma.
- Numbers in numerals (1, 2, 3), never spelled out ("one, two, three").
- Brand names capitalised. Categories lower-case ("salon",
  "restaurant", "gym" — not "Salons").
- City names always exact ("Rotterdam", "Den Haag", "Amsterdam Oost").
  Never "in the Netherlands" when a specific city would work.

### Per-post discipline

- Hook line is line 1. Visible without "see more".
- One CTA per post. Either a public report URL or
  `https://seoterrain.vercel.app/...`.
- No "follow for more". No "agree? yes/no". No "drop a 🔥".
- No hashtags except: 0–3 specific tags (`#LocalSEO`,
  `#KapsalonMarketing`, `#KleineOndernemer`). Never
  `#growthhacking`, `#AItools`, `#digitalmarketing`.

## 4 — Anti-patterns (the explicit "do not")

Stop and re-read this section if you're tempted by any of these.

| Anti-pattern | Why we don't |
|---|---|
| Daily posting "to feed the algorithm" | Solo builder can't sustain quality; signals desperation |
| Build-in-public daily updates | We are not building for tech-Twitter; SMB owners don't care |
| Engagement pods / follow-for-follow / mutual-like rings | Tanks credibility the first time someone notices |
| AI-generated post packs | The voice is the moat; outsourcing it kills it |
| "Year in review" / "what I learned in 2025" generic posts | Off-tone; everyone writes these |
| Career-advice posts | We don't have authority on that and it's off-brand |
| LinkedIn polls just for engagement | Manipulative; never seen one earn a real audit |
| Inspirational quotes / motivational posts | We are a tool, not a coach |
| "Drop a 🔥 if you agree" engagement bait | Off-tone |
| Tagging 10 people in the body of a post | Off-tone; cheap |
| Sponsored-feeling content with no labelling | Trust killer |
| Cross-platform mass repost (LinkedIn + Twitter + Instagram with identical copy) | Each platform deserves the right format or no presence |
| Posting in 3 languages "to maximise reach" | Dilutes voice; English with NL outreach copy is enough |
| Reposting own content within 30 days | Off-tone for a small audience |
| Cold-pitching in comments on someone else's post | Spam |
| Buying followers / engagement | One-strike termination of the brand |
| Mass DM after a like | Spam |
| Personal-brand "leadership philosophy" posts | We are not selling personal brand; we are selling audits |

## 5 — Content pillars (4 + 1)

The skill's framework recommends 3–5 pillars. SEO Terrain uses 4
content pillars + 1 reserved "promo" slot capped at 10% to avoid
sales-flooring.

| Pillar | % of content | What it does | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Teardown** | 35% | Show the product in action on a real audited business; concrete + visual | 1 every 2 weeks |
| **Pattern** | 30% | Distil what we learned across N audits in a category; expert positioning | 1 every 2 weeks |
| **Owner-voice** | 15% | Real-life proof + relationship moment; only when permissioned | When available |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | 15% | Trust + transparency about the product (scoring weights, why no AI, etc.) | 1 per month |
| **Promotional** | 5% | Direct product asks (new fix-guide, new category page) | When something ships |

### Why these four

- **Teardown** is the highest-trust pillar — concrete evidence beats
  any positioning statement.
- **Pattern** is the highest-authority pillar — only someone running
  many audits can produce these credibly.
- **Owner-voice** is the highest-credibility pillar — third-party
  validation, harder to fake.
- **Behind-the-scenes** is the highest-trust-without-customer pillar
  — useful even before there are real owners to quote.

### What's deliberately NOT a pillar

- "Industry news commentary" — too generic, every SEO creator does it
- "Personal life / day-in-the-life" — off-brand for a tool company
- "Founder journey" — defer to Month 6 once there's a journey
- "Lessons learned in startup-life" — wrong audience

## 6 — Reusable post structures

Four templates, one per pillar.

### Template 1 — Teardown post

```
[Concrete one-liner about a real audit we ran. Specific category + city.]

[Score + one-sentence summary of the business situation.]

Three things competitors are doing that they aren't:

→ [Specific competitor signal 1, 2-3 sentences explaining the gap]

→ [Specific competitor signal 2]

→ [Specific competitor signal 3]

[One-sentence timeline: how long it takes to fix + how long to see in rankings.]

[Direct link to the public report URL OR the matching category page.]
```

**Length**: 100–150 words. **Always ends in a real URL**.

### Template 2 — Pattern post (after N audits)

```
I've now run local SEO audits on [N] [category]s across [region].

Three problems show up every single time:

1. [Specific problem, 1 sentence explanation]

2. [Specific problem, 1 sentence explanation]

3. [Specific problem, 1 sentence explanation]

[One-sentence framing: how easy each is to fix, how long until results.]

If you run a [category] and want a free 60-second audit for your own:
https://seoterrain.vercel.app/audits/[cat]
```

**Length**: 80–130 words. **Always cites a real N**. Never inflate.

### Template 3 — Owner-voice post (permissioned)

```
[Time anchor: "Three weeks ago I audited a [category] in [city]."]

The owner did the three things the report recommended:
1. [Specific fix]
2. [Specific fix]
3. [Specific fix]

[Direct quote from the owner — short, real, permissioned.]

[1 sentence on what this unlocked for the business.]

[Direct link to public report URL OR seoterrain.vercel.app]
```

**Length**: 100–140 words. **Only post when the owner has permissioned
the quote.** Never compose a quote.

### Template 4 — Behind-the-scenes post

```
[A specific claim about how the product / scoring / philosophy works.]

[2-4 sentences explaining the why — concrete, no jargon.]

[A list of specific facts (weights, dimensions, features) that prove it.]

[A line connecting it to the audit experience.]

[Direct link, usually to /about or /scoring once that exists.]
```

**Length**: 110–160 words. **Never speculative**. Only what the
product actually does today.

## 7 — Engagement strategy (low-cost, high-trust)

The skill recommends 30 min/day. We do 30 min on **post days only**,
plus 20 min/day on non-post days. Total: ~2.5 hours/week.

### On post days

- Reply to every comment within 4 hours
- Use first names in replies
- If a comment is a real question, answer it concretely (not "great
  point!")
- If a comment is small-talk, like and move on
- DO NOT engage-prompt: "agree?", "thoughts?", "drop a 🔥"

### On non-post days (20 min)

- Comment on 2 posts per week from operators in the ICP (salon
  owners, web freelancers, dentists, real-estate agents). Real
  thoughtful comments — not "great post!".
- Like a few in-network posts (zero strategic intent — just human
  presence)
- Reply within the day to any DM (assume new connection = potential
  audit)

### Building relationships (long-game)

The skill recommends 20–50 target accounts. SEO Terrain version:

- Identify **10 LinkedIn accounts** in the SMB / local-marketing /
  small-agency space (NL preferred). Examples: founders of local
  fitness chains, indie web designers, salon-supplier
  representatives.
- Engage with their content twice a month. Always with substance.
- Share their content **with credit + insight**, not "this is great".
- Never DM-pitch before having had at least 3 substantive
  comment-thread exchanges.

### Things to never do

- ❌ Mass-DM after a like
- ❌ Cold-pitch in someone else's comment thread
- ❌ Tag people who didn't ask to be tagged
- ❌ "Hey 👋, would love to connect"
- ❌ Follow-back farming

## 8 — Weekly workflow (~2.5 hours total)

Practical, repeatable, fits around real work.

| Block | Time | Task |
|---|---|---|
| **Sunday evening** | 30 min | Pick 1–2 posts from the bank (Section 9). Lightly personalise: real numbers, current week's audits, real business names if permissioned. Schedule via LinkedIn native scheduler or post manually next morning. |
| **Tuesday 09:00** | 5 min | Post #1 goes live. Don't watch the feed obsessively. |
| **Tuesday 09:00–13:00** | 15 min (in blocks of 5) | Reply to early comments. |
| **Tuesday evening** | 15 min | Reply to the rest. Note any DMs that opened. |
| **Wednesday** | 20 min | Engagement-only: 2 thoughtful comments on target accounts. |
| **Thursday 09:00** | 5 min | Post #2 (if scheduled) goes live. |
| **Thursday 09:00–13:00** | 15 min (in blocks of 5) | Reply to early comments. |
| **Thursday evening** | 15 min | Reply to the rest. Note DMs. |
| **Friday** | 10 min | Weekly tally into the tracker (see KPIs). |
| **Total / week** | **~2 h 30 min** | |

**Batching rule**: don't write Tuesday's post on Monday at 22:00 because
you forgot. If Sunday evening isn't blocked off, the week's content
is at risk.

| Downstream artefact | Time |
|---|---|

## 11 — Shareable audit strategy (the real virality engine)

Forget "viral content". The strongest social loop SEO Terrain has is:

```
Audit produced
    │
    ├──> Founder shares anonymised teardown on LinkedIn
    │       │
    │       └──> Reader runs their own audit
    │
    └──> (If owner permissions)
            ├──> Public teardown page lives
            │       │
            │       └──> Audited owner posts it on their own LinkedIn
            │
            └──> Founder reposts the owner-voice version
```

### Tactical playbook

1. **Anchor every teardown post in a public report URL.** Don't use
   screenshots when the URL itself is a richer artefact.
2. **Anonymise unless permissioned.** "A salon in Rotterdam" is fine.
   "Salon X at street Y" without permission is a trust-killing
   misstep.
3. **Make permission frictionless.** When an audit ships and the
   owner replies, ask once: "Mind if I anonymise or use your name?"
   Two-line reply, done.
4. **Capture before-after.** When an owner ships a fix, ask for one
   metric they noticed (bookings, calls, profile views). One real
   number beats five abstract claims.
5. **Make the audited owner LinkedIn-share-ready.** When a teardown
   page goes live, send the owner a polished link with a suggested
   one-sentence post: *"My local SEO audit, fixed three things, and
   the difference."* Many will share. Some won't.
6. **One teardown page per month is enough.** Quality beats quantity
   here — the goal is one solid case-study per month, not a
   teardown farm.

## 13 — KPI dashboard (what to measure)

Single page, all in a Google Sheet. No social-analytics tooling
purchases.

| Metric | Frequency | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Posts shipped | Weekly | Manual tally |
| LinkedIn impressions per post | Weekly | LinkedIn analytics |
| LinkedIn comments per post | Weekly | LinkedIn analytics |
| LinkedIn reshares per post | Weekly | LinkedIn analytics |
| Profile views per week | Weekly | LinkedIn analytics |
| **Audits attributed to LinkedIn referrer** | Weekly | Postgres + UTM stamping |
| **DMs received from cold prospects** | Weekly | Manual tally |
| Posts that drove ≥ 1 audit | Monthly | Cross-reference UTMs |

**Vanity metrics to ignore entirely**: likes, follower count, post
impressions in isolation, "engagement rate". Only attributed audits
and qualified DMs matter.

### Decision rules

- **If a post gets 0 audits attributed AND 0 DMs**: it failed.
  Examine the hook. Don't blame the algorithm.
- **If a teardown post outperforms a pattern post 3:1 on attributed
  audits**: shift the ratio toward teardowns.
- **If 4 weeks pass without a single attributed audit from LinkedIn**:
  pause LinkedIn for 2 weeks. Re-evaluate whether time is better spent
  on cold email.
- **If profile views > 5x the audited-attribution count**: people are
  curious but not converting. Tighten the CTA copy.

## 14 — Tripwires (stop and re-read the rules)

Stop and re-read this document if any of these become true:

- Posting more than 3 times in one week
- Posting in 3+ languages
- Buying engagement (any form)
- Using AI to generate posts at scale
- Adding a LinkedIn company page before 5 paying customers
- Cross-posting identical content to TikTok/Reels
- Starting a podcast / newsletter / Discord
- Inventing a quote / customer / number
- Replacing real audit numbers with rounded approximations
- Writing a "year in review" / inspirational thread
- Starting a daily build-in-public log
- Following more than 50 new accounts in a week
- Outsourcing posts to a ghostwriter

The product is the message. Stop social-stunting and run more audits
if in doubt.

## 15 — Sustainability rules

Solo builder, multiple projects, real life. This needs to last 12
months without burnout.

1. **Sunday evening is sacred.** 30 min to pick + lightly personalise
   next week's posts. If Sunday is gone, skip the week — don't ship
   filler Monday.
2. **One channel, one cadence.** No second platform until LinkedIn
   produces ≥ 1 audit per post for 4 weeks running.
3. **30-min repurposing cap per post.** Anything more isn't worth it.
4. **No comparison anxiety.** Ignore competitor SEO creators posting
   3x/day. They have different goals.
5. **Quarterly review**, not weekly worry. Every 3 months, sit with
   the data and decide if anything should change. Don't change
   strategy because of one bad week.
6. **One vacation allowed.** Pre-write 2 posts before a 2-week break.
   Don't pretend social will keep itself running.

## 16 — Final principle

LinkedIn for SEO Terrain isn't a follower-count game. It's a
trust-signalling channel for cold-email replies and a long-tail
proof-page collection.

The job of every post is to make one audited owner forward the report
URL to their web person, or to make one cold-email recipient run their
own audit before replying.

If a post doesn't pass that test, don't ship it.

**The product is the message.** The audit is the magnet. Social is
just a megaphone.
