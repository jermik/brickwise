# YouTube Short — "30.9% Cleveland Yield"

**Target duration:** 38–42 seconds
**Format:** Vertical 9:16, 1080×1920
**Pacing:** Cuts every 2–3 seconds, no shot longer than 5s
**Voice:** Casual, founder, slightly skeptical, not hype

---

## Full spoken script (~100 words / ~40s at casual pace)

> "This tokenized rental property is showing a 30.9 percent yield.
>
> It's a single-family rental in Cleveland on Lofty. Tokens start at fifty bucks. Most people don't even know this market exists. Brickwise tracks 460 of them on Lofty and RealT.
>
> Here's the catch. Yields this high almost always mean something. Either the neighborhood is rough, occupancy is unstable, or the token is priced to clear because nobody's buying. My model scores it 78 out of 100. Solid, not top.
>
> This is either an opportunity or a warning sign. I post data like this daily. Follow if you want weird real estate data."

**Delivery notes:**
- Open with raised eyebrows on "30.9 percent yield." Slight pause after the number.
- "Here's the catch." → mid-cadence drop, more skeptical tone
- The three "either/or" possibilities → slightly faster pacing, listing them
- End line "weird real estate data" → small smile, casual sign-off
- **No em dashes anywhere in voiceover or captions. Use commas, periods, or new lines.**

## Pre-production checklist

- [ ] B-roll: Cleveland street(s), older row house, "for rent" sign — grab from Pexels (free, no attribution required for Shorts)
- [ ] Brickwise property page open in browser, scrolled to show yield + score
- [ ] `/analyzer` page loaded for the "460 properties" shot
- [ ] Phone on tripod or stack of books (eye-level)
- [ ] Window light or single ring light, no overhead
- [ ] Microphone close (Lavalier or phone close-mic, don't use rear mic)
- [ ] Wear plain solid color (no busy patterns — caption text reads better)

## Post-production checklist

- [ ] Captions auto-generated then manually fixed (auto-gen always mangles "Brickwise" and "RealT")
- [ ] No automatic music. Sound design: light ambient bed at -28 dB, slight rise on "the catch" beat
- [ ] Export 1080×1920, H.264, 30fps (60fps if device handles smoothly)
- [ ] Audio peaks at -3 dB, no compression that crushes the casual delivery
- [ ] Upload first to **YouTube Shorts** (better algorithmic fit for finance content than TikTok)
- [ ] Crosspost same export to TikTok and Instagram Reels (vertical works on all 3)

## What NOT to do

- Don't add a "follow for more" overlay until the last 3 seconds
- Don't say "guys" or "hey everyone" intro — go straight to the hook
- Don't add fake urgency ("you NEED to see this")
- Don't promise returns
- Don't use crypto/Web3 visual cliches (no glowing nodes, no Matrix code, no "blockchain" graphics)
- Don't use a beat drop or hip-hop track — finance audience runs cold from that
- Don't end on `#crypto` or `#web3` hashtags — they suppress reach for finance content on Shorts
