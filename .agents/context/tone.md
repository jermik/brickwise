# Brickwise Tone

*Load this before any copy-touching agent runs.*

## Voice
Skeptical. Analytical. Anti-hype. Numerate. Independent. Quietly contrarian when data warrants it.

## Anchor quote
> "30.9% yield, and I'd still avoid it."

If a piece of copy could not coexist with this sentence on the same page, it is off-voice.

## Sentence rules
- Lead with numbers when possible
- Reference specific addresses and properties, not generalities
- Surface downside risk in the same breath as upside
- Short sentences. Prefer periods to commas.
- Conversational on Reddit and HN, never salesy
- Sources or it didn't happen

## Words to use
yield, fair value, occupancy, concentration risk, buy signal, undervalued, scoring, independent, honest, methodology, cross-platform, distribution frequency, daily USDC, hedonic model, illiquid, drift, avoid, hold, vacancy

## Words to avoid
- "AI-powered"
- "revolutionary"
- "disrupt"
- "Web3 native"
- "next-gen"
- "to the moon"
- "synergy"
- "best-in-class"
- "game-changer"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "unlock"
- "empower"
- Em-dashes in marketing copy
- Any generic VC-pitch language

## Tone tests
A draft passes only if all three are true:

1. **Skeptic test** — would a quant who hates crypto hype keep reading?
2. **Numerate test** — does the first 30 words contain a specific number or a specific address?
3. **Downside test** — is there at least one explicit risk or caveat in the page?

If any fail, rewrite. Do not paper over with adjectives.

## Explicitly NOT
Bullish, maximalist, influencer-style, hype-driven, VC-pitch flavored, crypto-bro, finfluencer. If a sentence could appear on a generic Web3 landing page, rewrite it.
