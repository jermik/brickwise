# SEO Terrain Tone

## Voice
Practical. Grounded. Business-friendly. Honest. Useful over flashy.

## Anchor sentence
> "Your three biggest competitors are outranking you because of two specific things. Here they are."

## Sentence rules
- Plain English. No SEO insider jargon unless defined inline.
- Name competitors and pages by URL when calling them out
- Specific actions ("rewrite this title to include 'plumber in Cleveland'") beat abstract advice ("optimize titles")
- Short paragraphs. SMB owners read on phones in slow moments.
- Calm, not urgent. No countdowns or fake scarcity.

## Words to use
audit, competitor, ranking, visibility, local pack, Google Business Profile, citations, fix, week, opportunity, specific, practical

## Words to avoid
- "AI-powered"
- "10x"
- "supercharge"
- "dominate"
- "ranking secrets"
- "Google hates you"
- "algorithm hack"
- "leverage"
- "unlock"
- Anything that sounds like an SEO Twitter thread

## Tone tests
1. **Plumber test** — would a busy plumber understand the recommendation in one read?
2. **Specificity test** — does the advice name a specific page, URL, or query?
3. **No-jargon test** — is every technical term defined or replaced?

## Explicitly NOT
SEO-influencer chest-thumping, "secret tactic", agency theater, enterprise-platform polish, AI-tool wrapper energy.
