## Summary

Critical bug found and fixed across 16 files: string metadata titles containing `| Brickwise` were getting the root layout template (`"%s | Brickwise"`) applied a second time, producing doubled suffixes like `"Lofty Review 2026: Yields, Fees & Risks | Brickwise | Brickwise"` at 76–91 rendered characters — well over the 60-char Google truncation limit. The root cause is inconsistent use of `{ absolute: "..." }` vs plain string in the `title` field of `metadata`/`generateMetadata`.

2 pages also had short descriptions (<120 chars). Several long descriptions (>160 chars) were also trimmed in the same commits.

One issue is out of whitelist scope and needs human attention.
