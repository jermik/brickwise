## Summary

First SEO routine run. Baseline established. No regressions measurable (no prior data). The most impactful findings are:

1. **`/methodology` missing from sitemap** — a high-quality, well-written page is invisible to crawlers at priority level.
2. **Title double-suffix pattern** — many pages write `"... | Brickwise"` as a plain string without `{ absolute }`, which causes Next.js to apply the root layout template and render `"... | Brickwise | Brickwise"` in the `<title>` tag. **Needs live browser verification before fixing.**
3. **Description over 160 chars** on at least 4 pages — Google truncates at ~160 chars, wasting copy on high-intent queries.
4. **Title over 60 chars** on at least 9 routes — display truncation in SERP.

**Font loading**: All Google Fonts use `display: "swap"` via Next.js font optimization. FontShare (General Sans) uses the media=print non-blocking pattern with `display=swap` in the CDN URL. Both patterns are correct.

**Image dimensions**: `FallbackImg` renders a plain `<img>` with no `width`/`height`. This affects property images on homepage, rankings, city pages, and property cards everywhere. The containers use fixed heights (e.g. `h-[200px]`) which reduces CLS but doesn't eliminate it. Next.js `<Image>` would be the correct fix but is in `components/ui/` (blacklist).
