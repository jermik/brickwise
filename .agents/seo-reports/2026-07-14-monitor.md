## Double-Brand Title Pages (Finding 1 — detail)

Root layout (`app/layout.tsx`) sets `title.template: "%s | Brickwise"`. Pages that export `title: "… | Brickwise"` as a plain string get the template applied, producing `"… | Brickwise | Brickwise"` in the rendered `<title>` tag.

Pages confirmed affected by static analysis:

- `app/page.tsx`: `"Tokenized Real Estate Scored — Lofty & RealT | Brickwise"` → `"…| Brickwise | Brickwise"`
- `app/learn/what-is-tokenized-real-estate/page.tsx`
- `app/learn/lofty-review/page.tsx`
- `app/learn/realt-review/page.tsx`
- `app/learn/how-to-invest-in-tokenized-real-estate/page.tsx`
- `app/algorand/page.tsx`
- `app/algorand/[slug]/page.tsx` (via `const title = \`${project.name} … | Brickwise\``)
- `app/compare/best-fractional-real-estate-platforms/page.tsx`
- `app/compare/ark7-vs-lofty/page.tsx`
- `app/compare/lofty-vs-arrived/page.tsx`
- `app/compare/realt-vs-fundrise/page.tsx`
- `app/rankings/[category]/page.tsx` (all 4 categories via `\`${meta.title} | Brickwise\``)
- `app/city/[city]/page.tsx` (via `\`${cityName} … | Brickwise\``)
- `app/platform/[platform]/page.tsx` (via `\`${platformName} … | Brickwise\``)
- `app/market/[date]/page.tsx` (via `\`${u.title} | Brickwise\``)

Pages that correctly use `{ absolute: ... }` (no double-brand):
- `app/analyzer/layout.tsx` ✓
